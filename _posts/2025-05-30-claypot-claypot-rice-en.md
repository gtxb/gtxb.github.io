The essential of a claypot rice is always the well-cooked rice, although there are various toppings. The success of cooking white rice depends on adjusting heat properly.

The basic rules given here were inspired by the method of roasting coffee beans, because we cannot see the rice while cooking it in an opaque claypot with lid on, it's different from cooking other ingredients like blanching vegetables or stir-frying meat slices. Like the audible first crack in coffee roasting, we can recognize the stage when cooking claypot rice by hearing.

![claypot_white_rice](/assets/images/claypot-claypot-rice/teaser.jpg)

# Claypot white rice Recipe

A one-sentence recipe summary: soak one part rinsed rice in one part water for 30 mins then simmer and steam them for at least 20 mins.

## Ingredients and equipment

1 portion

| Ingredients   | by volume    | by weight |
|:--------------|-------------:|----------:|
| White rice    | 135ml     | 120g   |
| Water         | 135ml     | 135g     |
| Vegetable oil (optional) | 10ml      | 7g      |

  * Cantonese Claypot (⌀16.5cm size 550ml)
  * Stove top(1.8kW)

## Advance Prep

35 mins

  1. Rinsing: rinse the rice 3~4 times until water is clear to wash away excess starches on the surface
  2. Soaking: strain and soak the rice for about 30 min at room temperature

_(optional) Submerge the claypot in warm water, dry and rub a thin layer of oil over the bottom interior before using_

## Cooking

30~35 mins

  1. After preheat the claypot over low heat for about 1 mins, bring to a boil over medium-high heat but avoid exposing its bottom to direct, high flames, it takes about 5~10 mins
  2. Simmer over medium heat for 3~5 mins
  3. After hearing "pipapipa", heat over low heat then very low heat and steam the rice for 15~17 mins
 During this stage
    * Reduce the heat gradually：when the volume of "pipapipa" sound decrease, reduce the heat until it's just enough to maintain the high temperature inside the claypot
    * Rotate the claypot by a quarter-turn every 30 secs~ 1 min to ensure an even heat distribution
    * (Optional) After steaming for 6~8 mins, with the lid on, spoon oil and pour around the edge of the lid to pan-fry the scorched rice, the sizzling sound presents and then decrease gradually.
  4. Let it stand with the lid still on for 5~10 min. When it's quiet,  the lid, fluff the rice with chopsticks before serving.

# Basic Rules of Cooking Rice

## Rice-Water Ratio

The right **rice-water ratio** is the key to the success of cooking rice. However, it's a range, not a fixed number. The ratio given in the recipe is fixed based on several factors:

### Timing of absorbing water

Although we soak the rice to let it absorb water, the absorbing water actually starts from rinsing rice, not from the soaking step, in other words, the rice absorb about 10%[1] water of its own weight as soon as it contact the water. This means that the actual amount of water used in cooking rice = the amount that the rice absorbs when being rinsed + the amount of water added when soaking rice.

BTW, when cooking rinse-free rice in a rice cooker, if the rice-water ratio is the same as when cooking rinsed rice and the cooked rice is a little sandy, it's probable that the actual amount of water used in cooking decreased without the rinsing step and it's better to adding a little more water in this case.

[1] 10% is the average of the number that I measured by rinsing, patting dry the rice and weighting it three times

### Types of white rice

About the water in cooking rice, one part is absorbed by rice, another is evaporated during cooking.

There are two main types of rice, the long-grain Indica rice and the short-grain Japonica rice, this affects the rice-water ratio.
In general, we use more water to cook Indica rice than Japonica rice, as the former needs more heat and cooking time because of its higher amylose content, this means more evaporation during cooking, not because it can absorb more water, in fact, these two types of rice have the same capability of absorbing water.

### Cooking utensil

Using a broader pot or a lid that covers the pot not tightly leads to more water evaporation, which means more water should be added when cooking rice. The Cantonese claypot usually has a loose lid, as it's designed to pour oil easily into the claypot without opening the lid when pan-frying the scorched rice.

### Moisture added when serving rice

When adjusting the rice-water ratio, not only the water used in cooking rice should be considered, but also the moisture brought by sauce when serving rice. For example, the classic claypot rice, topped with sliced chinese sausage, is served with a liquid sauce, so a fluffy and slightly dry cooked rice is appropriate, as it can absorb a little the sauce after mixing them together before eating, it's moist just enough when eating.

It's the same when serving the white rice with other dishes, a fluffy rice makes a good accompaniment to the dishes with sauce, a dry stir-frying dish goes well with soft cooked rice.

### Personal preference

Although a typical claypot rice is made with long-grain rice, you can choose the type of rice and adjust rice-water ratio depending on your own preference.
The short-grain rice is usually soft and slightly sticky and the long-grain is fluffy. If you prefer the soft one, add 10%~20% more water when soaking and cooking rice or use equal parts short-grain and long-grain rice and mix them well before rinsing.

It's better to fix the rice-water ratio between **1:0.9 ~ 1:1.3 (by volume)** and to start from 1:1 then adjust 10% each time to find the one you prefer.

### Rice-water ratio by volume or by weight

Although measuring by volume is generally less accurate than measuring by weight, I recommend measure rice and water by volume in most cases. Firstly, measuring by volume is faster than by weight, it can be done even though with only a small cup as measuring device (find the detail in the next part "The right amount of rice"), not necessarily a standard volume measuring cup. Secondly, as mentioned before, the right rice-water ratio is actually a range, so this recipe doesn't require really precise measurements.

If you want to measure the ingredients with a scale, the rice-water ratio is about 1:1.0 ~ 1:1.4 by weight.

## The Temperature and Time Combination

### The right amount of rice

在合适的分量下，才有恰当的火力与时长组合：

  * 火力足够使其在适当时长内煮熟：若煮饭量过大且火力不足时，开火后煮很久才能沸腾，相当于浸泡时间拉长，同时由于加热时间拉长，水分蒸发相对多，煮至完全水干则需要停止加热，否则会糊底，但其中维持高温的时间偏短，尤其是靠近中心升温较慢的位置，成品可能既黏又夹生。

  * 为米粒膨胀预留空间：由于大米在浸泡吸水和煮熟后都会膨胀，若煮饭量过大，膨胀空间不足，底部的米粒也被上部的被压紧实，成品会因为膨胀不充分缺少蓬松感。

  * 适当的白饭与锅巴比例：如果煮饭量太少，相比体积，接触锅底的表面积大，意味着锅巴比例过高，导致整锅饭吃起来会有干硬的感觉。

那么如何确定使用的锅具适合煮多少大米？

我一般从**锅具容量的四分之一**开始：取一个小杯，向锅内装大米直至填满，数一下锅具能装的杯数，除以4即为放米的量。考虑到需要匹配火力，小锅倾向多煮一点，大锅则少煮一些。先以这个分量煮一次，根据成品品质反推烹煮是否恰当，再进行调整。

A good temperature and time combination

So how to determine the right amount of rice when cooking it in a specified claypot(or cast-iron pan/stainless-steel pot)?

### Two principles when adjusting the heat and cooking time

原则1：整体上火力逐渐减小

原则2：整体煮够20分钟

煮饭过程中随着水分越来越少，整体上火力越调越小，但也必须维持高温足够长的时间，通常至少20分钟，作为淀粉类食材的大米才能被煮透。

### Four stages in cooking rice

没有给出“大火加热5分钟后，小火煮15分钟”这样的描述，是因为**炉灶提供的火力大小不等同于锅内的实际受热**，尤其是第一次煮饭时，在无法确定火力大小是否恰当的情况下，如果只依赖烹煮时长调大小火很可能不准。应该结合锅内的实际受热情况，即“在特定火力下，是否能在固定时间内达到对应的状态”，来反推这个火力是否合适。比如，若从开始加热到沸腾共5分钟，那么这就是合适的“中大火”；若需要12分钟才能沸腾，在水量和分量都合适的情况下，这个“中大火”偏小了。也就是根据煮饭的状态，判断锅内实际受热，找调整火力的感觉，而非到了固定时长调整火力。

If you cannot identify whether the heat is enough, the cooking time is not reliable.
The temperature inside the claypot is not the same as the heat provided by the stove top  

这里把煮饭状态分成四个阶段，这四个阶段水温与水量的区别明显，个人推荐通过听声音区分不同的阶段。

煮饭期间虽然也能观察到其他特征，但不使用它们作为判断依据的理由是：

* 理论上烹煮作为淀粉食材的大米，需要维持90℃及以上的高温煮至少20分钟，但实际煮饭时并不方便使用温度计测量

* 能否看见从锅沿飘出的“白色热气”，取决于锅具周围的温度，因为只有水蒸气遇冷凝结才能被看到，随着加热的进行，周围温度升高，很可能会看不见。

* 频繁开盖会使水分蒸发过多以及锅内温度下降，即便使用透明的锅盖，可以看见米饭表面，但并不能观察到锅底的情况

* 若通过闻气味，米饭原本就滋味平淡，只有焦糊味会比较明显，但此时再调小火力已经晚了

即**通过听声音判断煮饭的阶段，调整火力使每个阶段维持恰当的时长**。

3.蒸煮

蒸煮阶段水分逐渐收干，直至关火前，米在极少的水分中半煮半蒸，调小火力至可维持蒸煮15~17分钟并且不糊底的程度。沸腾进行到后期，随着水分收干，滚水声逐渐减小，会有一小段比较安静的时间，然后逐渐变成“噼啪噼啪”的干脆响声，随着加热的进行，“噼啪噼啪”先是从依稀可以听见变为密集而剧烈，然后再次变得微弱，一般在此时调小火力，如果喜欢上色深的锅巴，可多煎一会儿再调小火力。因为声响越稀疏微弱，意味着水分越少，需要的火力越小，如果没有及时减小火力，锅内也异常安静时，可能会糊底。

这个阶段还有两项操作

3.1.转烧

土砂锅的材质决定了其导热速度慢，无法快速让锅子整体温度均匀，尤其在水分收干的这个阶段，务必经常调整加热的位置，对应的操作就是**转烧**。否则锅子可能会由于局部温差过大，进而开裂。

转烧一般每30秒到1分钟转一个角度，4~6个角度为一圈，角度和频率可以调整，整体均匀即可，最好转烧持续到关火前。

3.2.淋油

油脂的加入让同一锅米饭以不同方式加热：中间的米粒以残留的水分和蒸汽蒸煮，接触锅底的米粒则变为油煎。
淋油并非煮好饭的必需，但若想要锅巴焦香酥脆，油脂则是必要的，否则只是干硬。煲仔锅盖不严的锅盖加上弧形锅底，可以在不开盖的情况下，让沿锅边淋入的植物油均匀分布。适量的油脂可煎出焦黄的锅巴，同时锅底残油少，参考分量：1人份小锅（550ml）淋入2小勺（10ml）植物油。

4.焖蒸

关火不代表烹饪结束，此时利用余温烹煮米饭，锅里仍会有微弱的“噼啪”声，一般等到较安静时才开盖，通常5~10分钟。开盖后打散米饭，避免水汽遇冷凝结在米粒表面。若开盖太早，开盖瞬间会听见“哗啦”一声，水汽遇冷凝结在米饭表面，成品会有湿哒哒的感觉。
